Key anomalies

900 Block Washington Ave (Trespass) — Fridays at noon, nothing else
40 of 43 total incidents fall on Friday (93%). Peak window is 1pm (17) and 2pm (15 incidents). Zero 6am enforcement, zero weekends, and Thursday accounts for the entire non-Friday remainder (3 incidents). This location shares no behavioral signature with the other 13 trespass locations. Almost certainly a specific property or business with a predictable weekly enforcement schedule.
400 Block 4th St + 1000 Block 5th Ave (Trespass) — the 6am corridor
400 Block 4th St is 79% at 6am (45/57). 1000 Block 5th Ave is 91% at 6am (20/22). Both are 100% weekday. Add 600 Block 7th St (73% at 7am, 19/26), 500 Block 3rd St (62% at 7am, 16/26), and 1100 Block 6th Ave (45% at 6am, 13/29) and you have a geographic corridor being systematically worked in the same 6–7am window, Monday through Wednesday primarily.
10th St & Veterans Memorial Blvd (Camping) — every single incident on Monday
All 10 incidents at this intersection fall on Monday, peaking at 8am (5/10). The nearby 1000 Block Veterans Memorial is 100% weekday with 75% at 6am and Tuesday-heavy. Together they suggest a weekly two-day Veterans Memorial corridor clearance: Monday at the intersection, Tuesday at the 1000 Block.
300 Block Norway Ave + 100 Block 6th Ave (Shoplifting) — the weekend outliers
Every other shoplifting location is 76–93% weekday. Norway Ave is 67% weekend (12/18) with a sharp 8pm peak (5 incidents, 28% of its total). 100 Block 6th Ave is 82% weekend (9/11) on Sat/Sun evenings. Both likely represent convenience or bar-adjacent retail, not the strip malls and big-box stores that dominate the rest of the shoplifting data.
2200 Block 5th Ave (Shoplifting) — Friday concentration
Friday accounts for 32% of all incidents at this location (21/66), with Thursday adding another 23% (15/66). Over half the incidents fall on just two days. Peak hour is 1pm (14 incidents). Distinct from the broader shoplifting pattern where days are more evenly spread.
600 Block 4th Ave — two separate enforcement patterns on the same block
This address appears in camping, trespass, and paraphernalia. For camping and trespass the pattern is the 6am morning clearance. For paraphernalia, 50% of the 26 incidents cluster at 1–2pm on Tuesday and Wednesday — a distinct midday patrol pattern operating on the same block, likely hours after the morning sweep concludes.
